Keep It Looking Fresh

Tattoo Aftercare

Getting the tattoo is only half of it. I didn't know this before starting this project but how you take care of it after really determines how it looks long term. A lot of people ruin tattoos just by not following basic instructions. This page covers what to do and what not to do.

Important: This is general information — always follow what your specific artist tells you first. And if something looks infected, see a doctor, not the internet.

How the Healing Works

01

Days 1–3: It's an Open Wound

Right after getting tattooed your skin is basically an open wound. It'll ooze plasma and excess ink which sounds gross but is totally normal. Your artist will cover it — either with plastic wrap for a few hours or a medical bandage like Saniderm that stays on for a few days. Wash it gently 2-3 times a day with unscented soap, pat it dry, and put on a thin layer of unscented lotion. Don't rub it.

02

Days 4–14: The Itchy Part

The top layer of skin starts peeling like a sunburn and it itches like crazy. You absolutely cannot scratch or pick at it — this pulls out ink and can leave scars and patchy spots. Keep moisturizing lightly. The tattoo might look cloudy or dull during this phase which freaks people out but it's normal. The real color is underneath healing.

03

Weeks 2–4: Surface is Done

The top layer of skin is healed and it starts looking like a real finished tattoo. You can shower normally but still no swimming or soaking. Stay out of the sun — new ink fades super fast if you burn it. Keep moisturizing.

04

Months 2–6: Deep Healing

The deeper layers of skin (the dermis is where the ink actually lives) finish healing. Colors settle into their final form. From this point on the main thing you can do is wear SPF whenever it'll be in the sun. UV damage is the number one reason tattoos fade.

Quick Reference — Do's and Don'ts

When Do Don't
Day 1 Keep the bandage on as long as your artist said Take it off early or rewrap with regular plastic wrap long term
Days 1–14 Wash gently 2-3x a day, pat dry, apply thin unscented moisturizer Scratch or pick, use scented lotion, soak in water
First Month Wear loose clothing over it, keep it clean Go in the ocean or pool, let it get sunburned, let gym equipment rub on it
Always Put SPF 30+ on it before sun exposure, moisturize regularly Tan over tattoos, use tanning beds, skip sunscreen

Products That Work

  • Cleaning: Unscented Dial soap, Dr. Bronner's Baby Mild, any gentle fragrance-free soap
  • Moisturizing: Lubriderm Unscented, Curel Unscented, Hustle Butter (tattoo specific)
  • Bandaging: Saniderm or Tegaderm — your artist might apply this for you
  • Sunscreen: Any SPF 30+ broad spectrum — EltaMD and Neutrogena are popular ones

When to Actually See a Doctor

Some redness and swelling right after is normal. But if after 3 days it's getting MORE red or swollen instead of less, if the skin feels hot to touch, if there's pus, or if you develop a fever — that can mean infection and you should see a doctor. Infections are pretty rare if you follow aftercare but they do happen.

Long Term Care

After it's healed the main thing is just sunscreen. Sun is the biggest thing that fades tattoos over time. Other than that, keeping your skin moisturized helps keep the color looking good. Most artists recommend coming back for a touch up after a year or two especially if it got a lot of sun or is in a high-friction spot.
